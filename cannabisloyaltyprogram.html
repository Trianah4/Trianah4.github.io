Project: Cannabis Loyalty Rewards Program


Project Type: Client


Role: UI/UX Design


  • Research
  • Interaction Design
  • Visual Design

Overview

The client requested a wallet that provides a whitelabel, tailor made cannabis oriented loyalty reward app that meets the needs of dispensary owners by helping to boost revenue, build customer loyalty and help cannabis dispensaries create smarter cannabis marketing campaigns.

The Goal

Create an application for cannabis businesses that will allow users to link and manage payment to pay directly at stores, reward users with points for every purchase and allow cannabis dispensaries to continue to enroll, reward and support their customers.

Research Kickoff

To start off, a few questions needed to be asked. Who is the dispensary owner? Who is the dispensary member? Why would a member be using this app? Are there any limits we need to work with?


Starting with these questions, it became apparent what the client wanted to accomplish with this app and how these dispensaries that are looking for a loyalty program can use it to enroll, reward and support their customers to continue to foster and facilitate the customer journey.


Afterwards, Five participants will be given surveys to establish personas and gain user insight on what their goals and challenges are with the current way they receive SMS offers. Understanding the clients vision for this project and gaining user insight established what goals to bring to the forefront of the app and how to incorporate it throughout the user’s experience.

Competitive Analysis

An understanding was needed to exactly understand the type of application that needed to be designed to be intuitive to any users. So we venture out to see what other prominent cannabis marketing platforms are doing already and what goals they're not reaching.



Key takeaways

Two out of the three main competitors only offered SMS marketing, member management and automated campaigns to their users. They're limited with their customer loyalty which can cause difficulty retaining users.

Sprout offers all these features with their marketing platform but they fail to have exclusive brand deals.

Evaluating the Data

How much growth has the legal cannabis industry had in the last fews years?

According to Statista, the market has had an explosive growth in the United States since its recreational legalization. With Canada legalizing recreational use and the popularity of it in the United States, the market is projected to be close to reaching 63 billion dollars in value by 2024.


Source: Statista. May, 2021.

Conducting User Surveys

Five users were given surveys to establish the personas and gain customer insight for this project. The questions asked -


  • “If you are to purchase cannabis products, how much do you spend on average in the store?”

  • “Have you ever signed up for a Cannabis enrollment offer and why?”

  • “What do you dislike about how you receive/use current enrollment offers/points/rewards?”

  • How do you prefer to receive promotions/offers?






Key takeaways

The survey suggests that a majority of people who shop at dispensaries spend over $200+ every visit. With the SMS offers, they become enticed to shop more and save money on the brands they like and are allowed to try new products while saving money. This gives the customers a sense of being rewarded which allows these dispensaries to maintain customer retention.

While some of the customers like the way they receive their promotions/offers, others have a hard time using their rewards because they get buried in personal text messages, accidentally get deleted, or they forget when those offers expire.

From the insight, users also wanted a feature that would allow them to check in or scan for points right from the application.

After the Discovery Phase, Research Analysis, User Survey, and gaining user insight, this established the features for this app-


  • 1) Points Balance And Wallet Balance
  • 2) View Or Redeem Offers And Rewards
  • 3) Scan Card

Wireflow



Challenge 1

Points & Wallet Balance

The points and wallet balance was implemented to appear above the sub navigation menu across all screens to allow users to know where their balance at all times throughout the application.

Managing cards are located on the Scan screen. Each card has a quick menu to manage or add funds. Users are also able to register gift cards from their dispensary and are allowed to add gift cards or debit cards to their wallet.

Challenge 2

View Or Redeem Offers And Rewards

The sub navigation menu on the Home Screen, Users will find the information for their Offers, Rewards and History. This gives users an organized section with all of the information they receive from their dispensary. Whether it's simply viewing an offer or reward, or redeeming it, the process was made straightforward for users.

Once an offer or reward is available and redeemed, that information is sent to the rewards screen. This allows the user to see which offers or rewards are currently active and which have expired.

Challenge 3

Scan Card

A FAB is located on both the Home and rewards screen for quick access when opening the app. They’re two options available to the users when it comes to scanning, they can either scan their gift card or debit card and get awarded points with their purchase or the user pays in cash and then scan their points card only when making a purchase. History of points redeemed or collected is located in the sub navigation menu on the Home screen.

Design System

A Design System was created for consistency and familiarity for each client they offer their services to. The main typeface used was SF Pro Text, the typeface gives it a modern and professional feel to it.



Testing

Next, Five users who matched the users personas were recruited to test the prototype. The following tasks were given:

Where would you go in the app to claim your reward?

All users went directly to Rewards tab on the Home Screen.

Where would you go in the app to view your active and expired rewards?

The majority of users went directly to Rewards on the tab menu. The others went to profile first then to the Rewards tab.

Where would you go in the app to scan your card?

The majority of users went directly to Scan on the tab menu. A few users did browse the app before finding their card.

Where would you go in the app to add a new card?

80% of users went directly to Scan on the tab menu and were able to add a new card to their account.

The remaining 20% expected to add their card in profile and never went to the Scan Screen.

Takeaways

Ethos was a learning curve, it was a market that I was unfamiliar with so producing an application was a challenge I enjoyed. I had very little knowledge of the cannabis industry but with the help of the client and research, I was able to understand their vision and the challenges for this project. If there was more time, more research and usability testing would uncover additional pain points and would lead us to make better design decisions. Overall, the value of research and planning produces findings in the most beneficial way to our users within our constraints.
